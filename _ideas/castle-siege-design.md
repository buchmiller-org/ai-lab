# Castle Siege — Game Concept

**Genre:** Lane Defense / Tug-of-War  
**Setting:** Medieval, flat 2D side-view  
**Match Length:** ~2 minutes  
**Art:** All hand-drawn assets

---

## The Game

Two castles face each other across a single flat lane. You spawn melee units that march right toward the enemy castle; enemy units march left toward yours. First castle to fall loses. There are no obstacles, terrain, or other objects in the path — just two armies clashing in the middle.

## Currencies

- **Gold** — In-match resource. You start with a set amount and earn more passively each second. Spent to spawn units.
- **Iron** — Earned from enemy kills during a match. Spent on permanent upgrades between matches.

## Units (Prototype)

One melee unit type for the player, one for the enemy. Units auto-march and auto-fight. The player's only in-match action is deciding *when* to spawn.

## Enemy Waves

The enemy doesn't spend resources — units spawn in waves on a timer. Difficulty ramps gradually over the ~2 minute match (more units per wave, slightly tougher stats).

## Upgrades (Between Matches)

Spent with Iron. Each upgrade's cost increases after each purchase.

- **Unit HP** — Your soldiers get tougher.
- **Unit Damage** — Your soldiers hit harder.
- **Gold Income** — Faster passive gold during matches.

## Player Agency

Intentionally minimal for the prototype. You tap/click to spawn a unit when you can afford one. Strategy comes from spending timing — save up for a push, or trickle units out steadily?
